02·What I Noticed

Five specific things — from the doorway to the abandoned cart.

Mystery shop, no account access. iOS Safari. Standard first-time-buyer flow. What I saw is what your next customer will see.

MS-1 · SMS double pop-up

Two SMS subscribe prompts fired in the same session.

Duplicate trigger — either two independent sources (email capture + checkout capture) firing without deduplication, or a single trigger misconfigured to double-fire. Small thing, but the second prompt reads as spam to a customer who already said yes.

MS-2 · SMS code UX failure

The welcome code was 22 characters, unlinkable, and not auto-fillable.

The code (WELCOMEMNTD1557TPTGFM) arrived by SMS with no deep link and no tap-to-apply. To use it: open SMS → precisely select 22 characters → copy → open Notes → paste → return to browser → navigate back to cart → paste at checkout.

Impact. Six steps for what should be one tap. A material share of first-time buyers abandon between steps 3 and 4. This is the primary conversion lever leaking money.

MS-3 · Mobile navigation friction

Four sequential product decisions, each reloading the page.

Style → colour → size → length. Every step is its own page load, and back-navigation loses prior context. On a catalog structured for a desktop grid but served to a 70%+ mobile audience, this is a CRO problem before it's an email problem.

MS-4 · The bundle-vs-code conflict

Built the highest-AOV cart — the welcome code did not apply. No recovery.

Welcome offer is 15% off. Commercial policy excludes bundles. This exclusion was not disclosed at the offer stage — not in email, not in SMS, not on the bundle page. Cart abandoned at checkout. In the 72 hours that followed:

Zero abandoned cart email. Zero abandoned checkout email. Zero browse retargeting. Zero SMS recovery.

Impact. This is offer-induced cart abandonment. The commercial strategy acquires customers on a discount, then routes them to the one product line the discount excludes — with no recovery mechanism. Three simultaneous failures.

MS-5 · The strategic angle no one else has

Dads with daughters. A Knix-specific insight, invisible in the current program.

A meaningful share of MNTD's addressable audience was introduced to the brand through the Knix household — fathers, partners, brothers of Knix customers. Higher brand affinity, higher trust, receptive to bundling and gifting messaging. Nobody else in menswear can walk in with this angle. Right now, there is no onboarding question that captures it and no lifecycle branch that serves it.

Impact. This becomes the strategic vision of the program — Father's Day, birthdays, Christmas — grounded in first-party data no competitor can replicate.

04·The Gaps

Where MNTD is leaving revenue on the table — in order of dollar impact.

The scorecard tells you what is broken. This tells you the order I would fix it in, and the reason for the sequence.

Revenue leak · Tier 1

Offer-induced cart abandon on your highest-AOV product

The welcome discount excludes bundles, but bundles are the top of the cart. Every motivated new subscriber routes here, hits the exclusion wall, and leaves — with no recovery flow to bring them back. Fixing this is a bundle-code + cart-abandon-flow one-two.

Revenue leak · Tier 1

Zero cart / checkout / browse abandon coverage

Every DTC benchmark puts abandon flows at 15–30% of email revenue. MNTD's contribution here today is zero. The one flow with the highest ROI in the entire program does not exist yet.

Credibility · Tier 2

DMARC failing on 3 of 4 sends

Send-from address is info@wearmntd.com while DKIM authenticates on wearmntd.ca. Result: iCloud / Google DMARC verifiers log fail. BIMI blocked. Deliverability at risk as inbox providers tighten. One DNS change fixes it. Day-one task.

Conversion · Tier 2

SMS code that requires six steps to redeem

22-character welcome code, not tappable, not linkable, not auto-fillable on mobile. Shorten to MNTD15, wrap in a Klaviyo dynamic-coupon deep link that auto-applies at checkout, split by channel.

Strategic · Tier 3

No 1st-vs-2nd buyer segmentation

The welcome and re-engagement flows are the same email. First purchase is a barrier problem; second purchase is a habit problem. These need different copy, different offers, different cadence — starting with a 14-day and 30-day post-purchase trigger.

Strategic · Tier 3

The dads-with-daughters segment is invisible

The Knix relationship is a first-party-data moat no competitor has. One onboarding question — "how did you hear about MNTD?" — unlocks a gift-occasion calendar (Father's Day, birthdays, Christmas) that becomes an owned revenue channel. Introduce in month 2, mature over the quarter.
